# Competitor Brief: Empower (formerly Personal Capital)

**Last Updated:** January 2026
**Analyst:** FinTrack Product Team

---

## Overview

Empower (rebranded from Personal Capital in 2023 following its acquisition by Empower Retirement in 2020) is a dual-product company: a free personal finance dashboard (the direct FinTrack competitor) and a paid wealth management advisory service (the actual revenue engine). The free dashboard is a customer acquisition tool designed to identify high-net-worth individuals who may convert to Empower's financial advisory service, which typically requires a $100,000+ minimum investment and charges 0.49-0.89% AUM annually.

This business model has profound implications for the product: Empower's free dashboard is excellent for investment and net worth tracking (by design — they want to see your wealth so they can pitch management services), but basic to mediocre on budgeting features (they don't need you to budget; they need to see your assets).

---

## Key Features

**Free Dashboard Capabilities:**
- Automatic account linking via Plaid and proprietary integrations
- Net worth tracker across all asset classes (bank, investment, real estate, vehicles)
- Investment portfolio view with individual holdings, allocation, performance vs. benchmarks
- Fee analyzer (identifies hidden fees in investment accounts — a distinctive and genuinely valuable feature)
- Retirement planner with Monte Carlo simulation
- Cash flow tracker (income vs. expenses by month)
- Budgeting (basic — spending by category)
- Education savings planner (529 account planning)
- Net worth benchmark comparisons by age/income

**Wealth Management Service (Paid):**
- Dedicated financial advisor
- Personalized investment management
- Tax optimization strategies
- Estate planning support
- Minimum: $100,000 investable assets
- Fee: 0.49-0.89% AUM

**Notable Strengths:**
- Best investment tracking in the free personal finance category — bar none
- Retirement planning tools are sophisticated and genuinely useful
- Fee analyzer is unique — no other free app identifies hidden investment fees
- Handles complex financial situations well (multiple investment accounts, taxable vs. tax-advantaged, etc.)
- Free forever for the dashboard — no subscription required

**Missing/Weak Areas:**
- Budgeting tools are basic — not designed for detailed budget management
- No AI categorization — manual corrections required
- No credit score monitoring
- No bill calendar
- No savings rate dashboard
- Aggressive sales tactics for wealth management upsell — users complain about phone calls

---

## Pricing

**Free Dashboard:** Free (no subscription, no credit card required)
**Wealth Management:** 0.49% AUM for first $1M, 0.79% for $1M-$3M, 0.89% above $3M
**Minimum for paid service:** $100,000 investable assets

**Analysis:** Like Credit Karma, Empower's "free" model creates a price challenge for FinTrack. However, Empower's free tier exists to identify wealthy prospects — it is not designed as a budgeting tool. Users who want serious budgeting will find Empower insufficient. Users who primarily want investment and net worth tracking may find Empower's free tier sufficient.

---

## Target Market

**Primary for free dashboard:** High-net-worth individuals tracking complex investment portfolios
- Individuals with $100k+ in investment accounts
- Pre-retirees planning retirement drawdown strategies
- High earners who want portfolio performance tracking
- Users with multiple investment accounts (401k, IRA, taxable brokerage, HSA, 529)

**Primary for paid service:** Mass affluent ($100k-$10M investable assets) seeking financial advice

**Not well-suited for:** Active budgeters, people without significant investments, users who want spending control, users uncomfortable with financial advisor sales calls

---

## Strengths

1. **Investment Tracking Excellence:** Empower's investment dashboard is the gold standard in the free tier. Individual holdings, asset allocation, benchmark performance comparison, and risk analysis are all more mature than any other free or paid budgeting app.
2. **Retirement Planning:** The retirement planner using Monte Carlo simulation is genuinely sophisticated financial planning software that would typically cost hundreds of dollars per year as a standalone tool.
3. **Fee Analyzer:** Unique in the category. Empower identifies hidden fees in mutual funds and 401k plans — a feature that saves users real money and creates strong word-of-mouth among investment-focused users.
4. **Free with No Subscription:** For investment-focused users, getting portfolio tracking, retirement planning, and net worth monitoring for free is genuinely compelling.
5. **Data Breadth:** Empower connects to an extremely wide range of financial institutions — broader than most competitors, including 401k providers that Plaid doesn't cover well.
6. **Trust and Credibility:** As a subsidiary of Empower Retirement (one of the largest US retirement plan providers), the brand carries more institutional credibility than consumer-first fintech startups.

---

## Weaknesses

1. **Aggressive Wealth Management Upsell:** Empower's business model requires converting free users to paid wealth management. Users with >$100k in assets receive persistent phone calls, emails, and in-app prompts from financial advisors. This is the #1 user complaint and frequently cited in negative reviews.
2. **Poor Budgeting:** The budgeting tools are basic — category tracking exists but there are no budget alerts, no AI categorization, no budget goals, and no bill tracking. For users who want actual spending control, Empower is insufficient.
3. **Not Designed for Everyday Users:** Empower is designed for wealthy users with complex financial situations. Users without significant investment portfolios don't get much value from the most powerful features.
4. **No Credit Score:** No credit monitoring or credit score tracking despite being a comprehensive financial tool.
5. **Dated Interface in Parts:** While the investment dashboard is polished, the cash flow and budgeting sections feel afterthought-level compared to the investment tools.
6. **No Bill Calendar:** Empower does not track bills or provide payment reminders.

## Comparison to FinTrack

**Where Empower Wins:**
- Investment tracking — significantly more mature, individual holdings, performance, allocation
- Retirement planning tools — no FinTrack equivalent
- Fee analyzer — unique and genuinely valuable
- Institution coverage breadth (especially 401k providers)
- Brand credibility from Empower Retirement parentage
- Free price point for investment-focused users

**Where FinTrack Wins:**
- **Budgeting:** FinTrack has real budgeting with goals, alerts, and AI categorization; Empower's budgeting is rudimentary
- **No aggressive sales calls:** FinTrack's subscription model doesn't require upselling wealth management
- **Credit score monitoring:** FinTrack has it; Empower does not
- **AI categorization:** FinTrack's automated categorization; Empower requires manual
- **Bill calendar:** FinTrack tracks bills; Empower does not
- **Savings rate dashboard:** FinTrack-specific
- **User experience for non-wealthy users:** FinTrack is designed for typical earners; Empower is optimized for the wealthy
- **Price transparency:** FinTrack's $9.99/mo is honest; Empower's "free" is a funnel for AUM fees

**Strategic Positioning Against Empower:**
"FinTrack is built for you, not your advisor. Get comprehensive financial tracking — budgeting, net worth, investments, credit score — for $9.99/month, with no sales calls, no minimum balance requirements, and no hidden agenda."

**Win scenarios:**
- Users who tried Empower and got tired of advisor sales calls
- Users who want real budgeting tools, not just investment tracking
- Users without significant investment portfolios who feel Empower's tools aren't relevant to them
- Privacy-conscious users uncomfortable with their data being used to identify them as wealth management prospects

## Recent Updates & Trends

**2025-2026 Updates:**
- Empower Retirement completed rebranding from "Personal Capital" across all consumer touchpoints
- Expanded retirement planning tools with new income projection features
- Added limited AI-powered portfolio insights
- Mobile app updated with improved navigation
- Reduced minimum AUM for lower-tier advisory service to $100,000 (down from $200,000)

**Market Position:**
Empower occupies a well-defined niche that has limited overlap with FinTrack's core market. Their target user (high-net-worth, investment-focused) is largely different from FinTrack's target user (everyday budgeter seeking spending control). The overlap exists for FinTrack's investment tracking feature — users who want both budgeting AND investment tracking in one app.

**Strategic Threat Level:** LOW-MEDIUM
Empower is not a direct competitor for most FinTrack users. The threat is primarily for the subset of FinTrack users who have significant investment portfolios and might prefer Empower's more mature investment tools. As FinTrack's investment tracking matures (MVP shipping Q1 2026), this threat diminishes further. The more relevant competitive question is: can FinTrack eventually add enough investment tracking depth to convince Empower users that they don't need a separate tool?
