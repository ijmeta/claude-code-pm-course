# Sales Team Notes: Lost Deals Due to Investment Tracking Gap

**Compiled by:** Jessica Torres, Sales Operations
**Date Range:** Q3-Q4 2024
**Purpose:** Document deals lost or at risk due to lack of robust investment tracking features

---

## Executive Summary

Over the past 6 months, we've tracked deals where investment tracking availability was cited as a significant factor in the sales outcome. This document summarizes those opportunities.

**Total Deals Impacted:** 11 deals
**Total ARR Lost/At Risk:** $412,000
**Most Common Objection:** "We need to see our full financial picture including investments — FinTrack only covers spending and budgeting"

---

## Lost Deals

### Deal #1: Hartwell Capital Advisors (Referral Program)
- **Deal Size:** $92,000 ARR (B2B referral — advisor recommends FinTrack to ~80 high-net-worth clients)
- **Lost to:** Empower (formerly Personal Capital)
- **Sales Rep:** Marcus Chen
- **Notes:**
  > "The advisor loved the budgeting and categorization features. But their clients have significant brokerage and retirement accounts — Schwab, Fidelity, Vanguard. Without investment tracking, the app only shows half the picture. Empower won on investment portfolio view alone. Direct quote: 'My clients aren't going to use two apps for their financial life.'"

### Deal #2: TechPros Employee Benefits Partnership
- **Deal Size:** $67,000 ARR (corporate wellness partnership — provide FinTrack subscriptions to 500 employees)
- **Lost to:** Monarch Money
- **Sales Rep:** Ashley Rodriguez
- **Notes:**
  > "HR director was excited about the budgeting and bank sync features for financial wellness. But when we demo'd and employees asked about 401k tracking, we had nothing to show. Monarch had basic investment account syncing. That was the deciding factor — the company wanted employees to see their full net worth including retirement accounts."

### Deal #3: Summit Wealth Management Referral
- **Deal Size:** $48,000 ARR (referral program with RIA firm, ~40 client subscriptions)
- **Lost to:** Copilot
- **Sales Rep:** David Kim
- **Notes:**
  > "Wealth management firm wanted to recommend a tool to their clients for day-to-day spending visibility. But clients asked whether it integrates with their Schwab accounts for portfolio tracking. Copilot's investment sync sealed the deal. The advisor told us: 'When investment tracking is ready, call me — I genuinely prefer your UX and price.'"

### Deal #4: Freelancer Finance Community (Influencer Partnership)
- **Deal Size:** $28,000 ARR (newsletter + community partnership, estimated 250 conversions)
- **Lost to:** YNAB + Empower combo recommendation
- **Sales Rep:** Rachel Green
- **Notes:**
  > "Finance influencer with 85k followers. She loved the interface and AI categorization. But her audience — freelancers and solopreneurs — cares a lot about investment tracking alongside budgeting. She ended up recommending 'YNAB for budgeting, Empower for investments.' Told us: 'If FinTrack added investment tracking I'd switch my entire recommendation to you.'"

### Deal #5: Pacific Coast Financial Planning (B2B Referral)
- **Deal Size:** $58,000 ARR (financial planning firm, ~50 high-income client referrals)
- **Lost to:** Empower
- **Sales Rep:** James Wilson
- **Notes:**
  > "Financial planners really valued the clean UI and AI categorization — said it's better than Empower's clunky interface. But they couldn't recommend a tool that didn't show portfolio performance. Clients have $500k+ in investments that are invisible to FinTrack. Quote: 'Fix the investment gap and you have our whole book of business.'"

## Common Objections From Prospects

1. **"Where are my investment accounts?"** (heard in 75% of deals with income >$100k)
2. **"I can't see my 401k or brokerage in FinTrack"**
3. **"My financial advisor wants to see everything in one place"**
4. **"Empower shows my portfolio for free — why would I pay for something that doesn't?"**
5. **"I need one app, not two apps"**

## Bank Sync Reliability (Secondary Objection)

Beyond investment tracking, bank sync reliability came up in 4 deals as a secondary concern:

> "We had a prospect cancel their trial because their Chase account kept disconnecting. They said 'If the core feature doesn't work reliably, I don't trust the rest of it.'" — Sales Rep, James Wilson

> "One deal mentioned hearing about FinTrack's sync issues on Reddit before we even talked. They brought up Plaid reconnection problems proactively." — Sales Rep, Ashley Rodriguez

**Implication:** Sync reliability affects not just churn but new deal conversion. Every public complaint about bank sync creates friction in the top of funnel.

---

## Sales Team Recommendation

The investment tracking gap is no longer a "nice to have" objection — it's a primary disqualifier in 40%+ of our deals with income >$100k, particularly for:
- High-income individuals with significant brokerage/retirement accounts
- Wealth management and financial advisor referral partnerships
- Corporate employee wellness programs
- Users who discovered FinTrack as a Mint replacement and expect full financial picture

**Request #1:** Prioritize investment tracking public beta. Sales team has a pipeline of win-back opportunities worth $226k+ ready to close on launch day.

**Request #2:** Early beta access for high-value lost/at-risk accounts. Even view-only investment sync would unblock several pending renewals.
