REFERRAL PROGRAM PLANNING:
Zoe opened with the business case: our current organic referral rate is ~12% of new signups, but it's entirely unstructured — no incentive, no tracking, no attribution. Industry benchmarks for personal finance apps with a formal referral program are 25-35% of new signups. If we can move from 12% to even 20%, that's roughly 150 additional paid subscribers per month at zero acquisition cost.

Proposed program structure: "Give a month, get a month." Referring user gets one free month ($9.99 credit) when a referred friend becomes a paid subscriber. Referred friend gets their first month free. Simple, bilateral, no confusing tiers.

Celine ran the math: assuming a 4% conversion rate on referral invites sent, at current user base of ~3,800 paid subscribers, we'd expect ~380 invites sent in month 1 leading to ~15 new paid subscribers. Not transformative on its own but compounds over time and improves word-of-mouth culture.

Maya's concern: launching a referral program while churn is elevated sends a mixed signal — we're asking users to recommend us before we've fixed the reliability issues they're complaining about. She suggested delaying the launch until March, after the bank sync reliability improvements ship. Zoe agreed.

CONTENT CALENDAR PLANNING:
Ben presented the Q1 content plan. Current blog traffic is low (2,400 monthly visits) and almost entirely SEO-driven with no social distribution strategy. Proposed shift: fewer but longer-form pieces targeting high-intent search terms, paired with social distribution on LinkedIn and TikTok.

Top content themes for Q1:
1. "How to build your first budget" (high search volume, targets free trial signup)
2. "YNAB vs FinTrack vs Monarch Money: honest comparison" (competitive positioning)
3. Tax season content series: "What to track all year for easier taxes" (seasonal relevance)
4. Personal finance success stories from FinTrack users (social proof, shareable)

Celine suggested testing short-form TikTok/Reels content showing FinTrack features in 30-60 second demos — this format has worked well for YNAB and Copilot. Ben flagged capacity: content team is just him right now, so this would require either a contractor or a reduced blog schedule.
