USER RESEARCH DEBRIEF - DEBT PAYOFF FEATURE NEEDS
Date: January 15, 2026
Attendees: Priya Sharma (Head of Product), Jordan Lee (PM, Core Experience), Kai Nguyen (Design Lead), UX Researcher (Mia Torres)

RESEARCH GOAL: Understand what FinTrack users with significant debt need from a debt payoff feature. 6 user interviews conducted Jan 8-13. Participants had $8k-$74k in outstanding debt (credit cards, student loans, auto loans, personal loans).

KEY FINDINGS:

Finding 1 - Debt feels invisible until it's overwhelming:
5 of 6 participants said they avoid looking at their total debt because "it's depressing." They want help making it feel manageable, not just visible. A simple "you owe $X" number is demotivating. What motivates them: progress visualization, milestone celebrations, showing the end date.

Finding 2 - They want a payoff strategy, not just a tracker:
All 6 participants explicitly asked for payoff strategy recommendations — specifically, the avalanche method (highest interest first) vs. snowball method (smallest balance first). Several mentioned seeing these methods on YouTube/Reddit and wanting FinTrack to apply them automatically given their specific accounts.

Finding 3 - The math is confusing and trust is fragile:
Participants didn't trust manually entered interest rates to stay current. "What if my rate changes? Will FinTrack know?" They want FinTrack to pull interest rates directly from their linked accounts rather than requiring manual input.

Finding 4 - They want to see how extra payments change the outcome:
4 of 6 participants wanted an interactive "what if" calculator: "If I pay $200 extra per month, when will I be debt-free?" This was described as highly motivating — seeing the debt-free date move closer when they increase payment amount.

Finding 5 - Integration with budget is essential:
All participants said a debt payoff feature only makes sense if it connects to their monthly budget. "Show me how much I have left after bills and food that I could put toward debt." The debt planner needs to be aware of their spending, not a separate isolated tool.

QUOTE HIGHLIGHTS:
- "I don't need you to tell me I'm in debt. I need you to tell me how to get out."
- "Show me the light at the end of the tunnel."
- "My credit card APR is 24%. If FinTrack could just tell me to pay that one off first, I'd do it."
