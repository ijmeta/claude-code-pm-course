CUSTOMER SUCCESS REVIEW - Q4 2025 CHURN ANALYSIS
Date: January 8, 2026
Attendees: Maya Patel (CEO), Priya Sharma (Head of Product), Jordan Lee (PM, Core Experience), Customer Success Lead (Chris Walton), Data Analyst (Fatima Osei)

BACKGROUND:
Monthly churn ticked up from 2.1% in October to 2.9% in December — the highest it's been in 18 months. Maya opened the meeting noting this is the #1 concern heading into Q1 and needs a clear action plan.

CHURN DATA (Q4 2025):
Fatima presented exit survey analysis from 89 churned users in Q4:
- 31% cited "bank sync problems / unreliable data" as primary reason
- 24% said "missing features I need" (most commonly: investment tracking, debt payoff planner)
- 19% cited cost — felt $9.99/mo wasn't worth it given reliability issues
- 14% switched to a competitor (Monarch Money mentioned most often, followed by Copilot)
- 12% "stopped actively budgeting / life circumstances"

TOP SUPPORT TICKET THEMES (Q4):
Chris walked through the top five ticket categories by volume:
1. Bank sync disconnections (34% of tickets) — Chase and Wells Fargo dominant
2. AI miscategorization (21%) — Amazon and subscription services most affected
3. Missing/delayed transactions (18%) — Plaid latency issues
4. Mobile bugs (14%) — iOS crash, notification failures
5. Billing/plan questions (13%) — mostly downgrades and export requests

DISCUSSION:
Priya noted that themes 1, 3, and 4 are all fundamentally reliability issues — users don't trust the data. Chris added that in retention calls, the most common objection is "I can't tell if the numbers are right." Maya emphasized that no amount of new features fixes a reliability perception problem — we need to win back trust first.

Jordan proposed a "Data Confidence" initiative: improving sync status visibility, adding transaction verification nudges, and proactively communicating when known sync issues are affecting accounts. Team agreed this is the right framing.

ACTION ITEMS:
- Chris to build a win-back campaign targeting Q4 churned users once reliability improvements ship (target: March)
- Fatima to segment churn data by plan age (new vs. long-term users) by Jan 12
- Priya to add "Data Confidence" as a named initiative in Q1 roadmap
- Jordan to draft feature brief for proactive sync status communication
- Maya to review and approve Q1 retention strategy by Jan 15

DECISION: Q1 theme is reliability and trust. New feature development subordinate to reliability fixes until churn rate returns to below 2.0%.
