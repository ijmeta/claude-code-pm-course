# User Interview: Marcus Thompson

**Date:** February 4, 2025
**Role:** Software Engineer, remote (Austin, TX)
**FinTrack User Since:** August 2024
**Interview Duration:** 30 minutes

---

## Background

Marcus is a 28-year-old software engineer at a fintech startup. He's been using FinTrack for about six months, primarily to track his savings rate as he works toward a down payment on a house. He has a Fidelity brokerage account, a Roth IRA, and his employer's 401k plan.

---

## Key Quotes

**On investment tracking:**
> "I connect all my bank accounts and they work great. But my Fidelity brokerage and my 401k are basically black boxes. I can see the balance, but I can't see my allocation, my performance, or how I'm doing against a benchmark. I have to log into three separate sites to get that picture."

**On what he actually wants:**
> "Just show me my total portfolio, my allocation across everything, and whether I'm beating the S&P. That's it. I'm not asking for financial advice, I just want the data in one place. Right now FinTrack is great for spending and useless for investing."

**On the savings rate dashboard:**
> "That's genuinely my favorite feature. I have a target of 25% and I check it every Monday. When I'm under, I think about what happened that month. When I'm over, it feels great. It's become like a personal KPI."

**On AI categorization:**
> "It's pretty good but I have a couple frustrations. My GitHub Copilot subscription keeps getting put under 'entertainment' instead of 'professional development.' And Amazon orders are a mess - sometimes it's groceries, sometimes it's electronics, sometimes it's books. It doesn't look at the item, just the merchant."

---

## Feature Requests

1. Investment portfolio view with allocation breakdown across all connected accounts
2. Performance benchmarking against S&P 500 or custom benchmark
3. AI categorization rules - let users set permanent rules for recurring merchants
4. Net worth projection - "at this savings rate, where will I be in 5 years?"

---

## Willingness to Pay

> "I'm on the monthly plan. If investment tracking actually worked well, I'd switch to annual in a heartbeat. I'm currently paying for Empower separately just for the investment dashboard. FinTrack could replace that if they built it out."

---

## Summary

Power user focused on investment tracking and savings rate. The spending side works well for him; the investment side is a major gap causing him to pay for a second tool. High loyalty but at risk of consolidating on a competitor that does both well. Clear opportunity to eliminate a use case for a competing product.
