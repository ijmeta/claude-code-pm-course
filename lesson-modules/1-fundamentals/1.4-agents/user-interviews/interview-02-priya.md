# User Interview: Priya Nair

**Date:** February 6, 2025
**Role:** Stay-at-home parent / former HR manager (Charlotte, NC)
**FinTrack User Since:** October 2024
**Interview Duration:** 28 minutes

---

## Background

Priya, 34, left her HR manager job 18 months ago after her second child was born. Her husband Raj works in pharmaceutical sales. They have two kids (ages 2 and 5), a mortgage, and are trying to get their budget under control on a single income. She manages all household finances and connected all their accounts to FinTrack when a friend recommended it.

---

## Key Quotes

**On the bank sync experience:**
> "The first week was a nightmare. Three of our five accounts wouldn't connect. I spent two hours with support trying to get our credit union to link. They finally got it working but it disconnects every few weeks. I dread that notification that says 'account needs attention' because it means another 20 minutes troubleshooting."

**On the biggest pain point:**
> "When an account disconnects we're missing transactions. So my budget numbers are wrong and I don't know it until I see something weird. I once thought we were $400 under our grocery budget when actually we'd been over for three weeks and just not seeing the charges. That's worse than not having an app at all."

**On what she values:**
> "When it's working? The budget alerts are amazing. Getting a text that says I'm at 90% of my dining budget on the 22nd means I can actually do something about it. That kind of real-time feedback is why I keep renewing."

**On the mobile experience:**
> "This is mostly a phone app for me. I'm not at a computer much. Checking FinTrack while the kids are at the park, or in the pickup line at school. The app is generally good but the loading times bother me. The net worth screen in particular takes forever on my older iPhone."

---

## Feature Requests

1. More reliable bank sync - especially for smaller regional banks and credit unions
2. Manual transaction entry as a backup when sync fails
3. Automatic alerts when an account goes more than 3 days without syncing
4. Household budget mode - she wants Raj to get the same budget alerts, not just her

---

## Willingness to Pay

> "We're on monthly right now. With a single income I think about every subscription. If the bank sync was reliable, I'd switch to annual to save the few dollars. But I'm not going to commit to a year if it keeps breaking."

---

## Summary

Primary household financial manager dealing with recurring bank sync reliability issues. When the product works, she's an enthusiastic user. When sync breaks, she loses trust in the data entirely. Retention is dependent on sync reliability. Represents a large segment of users managing household finances on a phone; performance and reliability are table stakes for this persona.
