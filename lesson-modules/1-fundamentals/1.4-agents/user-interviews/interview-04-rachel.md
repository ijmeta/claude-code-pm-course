# User Interview: Rachel Torres

**Date:** February 13, 2025
**Role:** Marketing director (San Francisco, CA)
**FinTrack User Since:** June 2024
**Interview Duration:** 27 minutes

---

## Background

Rachel is 31 and recently married. She and her husband Diego both work full-time and manage their finances through a combination of separate accounts and one joint account for shared expenses. She set up FinTrack and has been trying to get Diego more involved. She's technically savvy and has opinions about UX.

---

## Key Quotes

**On the couples/shared finances problem:**
> "The app is clearly designed for one person. I set it up, I connected our accounts, I manage the budgets. Diego has the app on his phone and theoretically can see everything. But it's my FinTrack account. If Diego wants to change a budget category or add a note to a transaction, he has to ask me. We're supposed to be equal partners."

**On what equal partnership would look like:**
> "Two user accounts that share the same financial data with the same permissions. Not one owner and one viewer. We should both be able to set budgets, both get alerts, both categorize transactions. Right now it's my financial dashboard that Diego is allowed to look at. That's not a couples tool, that's a one-person tool with a read-only guest mode."

**On notification issues:**
> "The alerts are too noisy and they only go to me. I get a notification every time a transaction comes in. That's like 20 notifications a day. I've turned most of them off and now I'm probably missing the ones that matter. I want two settings: 'alert me immediately when we hit 90% of any budget' and 'send a weekly summary of everything else.' That's it."

**On what's working:**
> "The spending insights are genuinely useful. Seeing last month's breakdown in categories and comparing to the month before is exactly the kind of thing I'd spend an hour in a spreadsheet building. It saves time and it's actually pretty accurate. The bill calendar is also great - we have so many subscriptions and having them all visible is reassuring."

---

## Feature Requests

1. True co-owner model for couples - two full accounts sharing one household dataset
2. Per-person notification settings within a shared account
3. Budget threshold alerts that go to both partners simultaneously
4. Transaction commenting/annotation so both people can add context

---

## Competitive Pressure

> "Monarch Money has a couples feature that a friend uses. I looked at it and the shared account model is better. If FinTrack doesn't build this, I might switch when our annual plan renews. The rest of FinTrack is good but this is a real quality of life issue for us."

---

## Willingness to Pay

> "We'd pay more for a proper couples plan. Honestly, $14.99 or even $19.99 a month for two full users sharing one account feels fair. Right now it's $9.99 for one real user and one kind of broken guest experience. That math doesn't work."

---

## Summary

Sophisticated user with a clear, well-articulated couples use case that FinTrack doesn't serve well. The core product - spending insights, bill calendar - works well. The account ownership model is the failure point. She's aware of Monarch Money as an alternative with better couples support and is at real churn risk at renewal. A couples/household plan at a higher price point would likely retain this segment.
