# User Interview: David Kowalski

**Date:** February 10, 2025
**Role:** High school history teacher (Chicago, IL)
**FinTrack User Since:** March 2024
**Interview Duration:** 25 minutes

---

## Background

David is 45, married with one teenager at home. He's been using FinTrack for nearly a year. His wife handles their investments; he focuses on day-to-day spending and the household budget. He's an experienced FinTrack user but describes himself as a "casual" user - he checks in a few times a week but doesn't dig into analytics much.

---

## Key Quotes

**On the mobile app:**
> "I use the app more than the website, probably 80/20. The thing that drives me crazy is the transaction categorization lag. I buy something with my debit card and the transaction shows as 'uncategorized' for like four or five hours. I want to see it categorized right away so I know where it landed."

**On AI categorization accuracy:**
> "It's pretty good overall. But I have this one recurring issue - I use a local farm CSA, so I get a charge from 'Green Valley Farm' every other week. FinTrack keeps categorizing it under 'agriculture' or sometimes 'business expenses.' It's groceries. I've recategorized it probably eight times and it keeps forgetting."

**On what he'd change:**
> "I want to train it. Like, 'for THIS merchant, always use THIS category.' Set it once, done forever. Right now it seems to use my corrections for a little while and then reset. That's incredibly frustrating for a feature that's supposed to be smart."

**On features he doesn't use:**
> "The net worth tracker is there, I look at it occasionally. Investments are all my wife's domain. The credit score thing - I glance at it. Mostly I'm here for the budget and the spending breakdown. That's the core value for me."

---

## Feature Requests

1. Permanent merchant-level categorization rules ("always categorize Merchant X as Category Y")
2. Faster AI categorization - within minutes, not hours
3. Simpler notification preferences - too many options, hard to configure
4. A "recently miscategorized" flag to make it easier to find and fix AI errors

---

## Competitive Pressure

> "My brother uses YNAB and swears by it. I've tried it twice and find it too labor-intensive. FinTrack is easier but I feel like YNAB would actually learn my patterns better. If FinTrack's AI got smarter about remembering my corrections, I'd have no reason to consider switching."

---

## Willingness to Pay

> "I'm on annual. I think it's reasonable. Teachers don't make a lot but $79 a year is less than two dinners out. If the AI categorization got significantly better, it would feel like a clear upgrade."

---

## Summary

Long-term user with a specific, recurring pain point around AI categorization persistence. The feature works initially but doesn't retain user corrections, causing repeated frustration. Strong retention signal (annual plan, low churn risk) but frustration is building around this one issue. Merchant-level categorization rules would resolve his primary complaint. YNAB is a reference point - FinTrack wins on ease of use but risks losing to perceived intelligence gap.
