# User Interview: James Okafor

**Date:** February 18, 2025
**Role:** Pharmacist (Atlanta, GA)
**FinTrack User Since:** January 2024
**Interview Duration:** 32 minutes

---

## Background

James is 38 and has been using FinTrack for over a year - one of the longer-tenured users in the cohort. He has significant student loan debt from pharmacy school ($87,000 remaining), a mortgage, and a young child. He manages finances carefully and uses FinTrack's debt payoff tools extensively. He also manages a small investment portfolio through Schwab.

---

## Key Quotes

**On the debt payoff tools:**
> "The debt payoff planner is the reason I'm still subscribed a year later. I've tried four or five budgeting apps over the years. The debt visualization - watching that number go down and seeing how extra payments affect the payoff date - is uniquely motivating. I check it after every loan payment. It's like a game I'm winning."

**On what's missing:**
> "I want the debt payoff goal to connect to everything else. Right now the debt planner is kind of its own thing. But my debt payoff strategy affects my budgets, my savings rate, my investment contributions. I want FinTrack to say: 'Given your debt payoff goal, here's what your monthly budget should look like.' Right now I'm calculating that manually."

**On investment tracking:**
> "My Schwab account connects but it basically just shows a balance. I have ETFs, a few individual stocks. I want to see performance over time, my allocation, whether I'm diversified. FinTrack shows me the number but doesn't help me understand it. I have to log into Schwab for any real analysis."

**On the mobile experience:**
> "I'm mostly on mobile. The app is good overall but the debt payoff section doesn't work great on small screens. The charts are hard to read on my phone. I've ended up doing the debt analysis on my laptop even though I prefer using the app for everything else. The chart sizing and the touch targets feel like they were designed for a bigger screen."

**On the feature he'd pay most for:**
> "A unified financial plan. Not just 'here's your budget' and 'here's your debt.' Tell me: given that I want to be debt-free in 4 years and retire comfortably at 62, here's what my savings, spending, and debt payments should be each month. That's the product I'd pay $20 or $25 a month for. FinTrack has all the data - it just doesn't do the synthesis."

---

## Feature Requests

1. Integrated financial plan that connects debt payoff, savings goals, and investment targets
2. Investment performance tracking with allocation breakdown (not just balance)
3. Mobile-optimized debt payoff charts with better touch targets
4. Milestone celebrations for debt payoff progress (paid off a loan, hit 50% remaining, etc.)

---

## Willingness to Pay

> "I'm on annual. For $79 the debt payoff tools alone are worth it. But I think about the $25/month I pay for my financial advisor and wonder why FinTrack can't do more of what she does. If FinTrack built out a proper financial planning layer, I could see paying $15-20 a month and reducing my advisor visits."

---

## Summary

Highly engaged long-term user whose primary use case is debt payoff tracking. Strong emotional connection to the debt visualization tools. Sees FinTrack as a potential replacement for parts of his financial advisor's services if the planning features mature. Investment tracking and mobile chart experience are the two functional gaps. High advocacy potential - enthusiastic about the product - but wants it to go deeper on the planning side. Represents a premium segment willing to pay more for more sophisticated features.
