# User Interview #5: Debt Payoff Persona

**Participant:** Danielle Reyes
**Role:** Registered Nurse
**Age:** 30
**FinTrack Plan:** Monthly ($9.99/mo)
**Date:** February 12, 2025
**Interviewer:** Senior PM
**Duration:** 33 minutes

---

## Background

Danielle graduated nursing school with $54,000 in student loans and picked up $8,000 in credit card debt during a rough patch two years ago. She's been aggressively paying down debt for the past 14 months and started using FinTrack after her coworker showed her the debt payoff tracking features. She's detail-oriented and tracks everything.

---

## Interview

**Q: What was your situation before you started using FinTrack?**

Danielle: "I knew I had debt. I knew it was a lot. But I didn't have a plan - I was just paying the minimums plus whatever extra I could afford each month. I didn't know exactly what my total debt was across all the accounts, or what order to pay things off. I'd heard of the avalanche and snowball methods but never actually ran the numbers."

**Q: How did FinTrack change that?**

Danielle: "The debt payoff planner changed everything. I put in all my loans and credit cards - the balances, interest rates, minimum payments - and it modeled out both the avalanche and snowball strategies. The difference in total interest paid was like $3,400. I never would have run that calculation myself. That one feature made me subscribe immediately."

**Q: Walk me through how you use the debt payoff tools.**

Danielle: "Every payday I open the app and see where I stand. I have a dashboard showing my total debt balance going down over time - it's satisfying to watch that number shrink. I have my payoff date projected out and I can see how extra payments move that date. Last month I threw an extra $200 at my credit card and it moved my payoff date by almost 3 months. That kind of instant feedback keeps me motivated."

**Q: What's working really well?**

Danielle: "The progress visualization. I'm a visual person. Seeing the chart of debt going down is way more motivating than a spreadsheet. Also the bill calendar - I have six different loan payments due on different days and the calendar keeps me from missing any. I set up alerts for three days before each due date."

**Q: What's frustrating or missing?**

Danielle: "Two things. First, my student loans are through FedLoan and they keep disconnecting in FinTrack. I've had to manually enter the balances like four times. It's annoying because that's the most important account to track. I've talked to support twice - they said FedLoan has a finicky API. Fine, but I need it to work."

**Q: What's the second thing?**

Danielle: "There's no way to track my debt payoff progress against a target date. Like, I set a goal to be debt-free by my 33rd birthday. I want a big countdown on my dashboard. I want FinTrack to alert me if I'm falling behind on that pace. Right now I have to do the math myself. The tools are there but they don't connect into a goal-tracking system."

**Q: How about the spending side of FinTrack?**

Danielle: "I use that too. Paying off debt is mostly a math problem but I also need to keep spending in check to free up extra cash. I have a pretty strict budget and the real-time tracking keeps me honest. When I see I'm at $180 out of a $200 dining budget on the 20th, I cook at home the rest of the month. That extra $100-150 goes straight to debt."

**Q: Have you looked at the credit score monitoring?**

Danielle: "Yes! My credit score has gone up 65 points since I started paying down the credit card debt. FinTrack shows me the factors - utilization is what's moving the score the most. It's really satisfying to watch. I share screenshots of my debt paydown progress and credit score improvement with my sister who's trying to do the same thing."

**Q: What would make FinTrack better for people focused on debt payoff?**

Danielle: "A debt payoff community or comparison - like, am I paying down debt faster or slower than people with similar situations? Not to compete, just for context. And better celebration moments. When I paid off my credit card entirely, the app just... updated the number. I wanted confetti or something. Make the wins feel bigger."

**Q: How do you feel about the price?**

Danielle: "Honestly I think about canceling sometimes when money is tight. But then I remember how the debt planner helped me save $3,400 in interest and I can't justify canceling over $10 a month."

---

## Key Insights
- Debt payoff planner with avalanche/snowball comparison is primary adoption driver
- Debt balance trend visualization is highly motivating - emotional resonance matters
- Student loan bank sync (FedLoan) is unreliable - recurring reconnection issue
- Goal-date tracking for debt payoff is missing - users want to track against a specific target date
- Bill calendar + due date alerts are highly valued for multi-loan management
- Credit score monitoring creates a positive feedback loop alongside debt paydown
- Celebration/milestone moments are weak - opportunities to increase emotional engagement
- Spending budget discipline and debt payoff are tightly linked in user mental model
