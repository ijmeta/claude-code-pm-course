# User Interview #1: Young Professional / Home Buyer Persona

**Participant:** Jordan Chen
**Role:** Software Developer
**Age:** 27
**FinTrack Plan:** Monthly ($9.99/mo)
**Date:** February 3, 2025
**Interviewer:** Senior PM
**Duration:** 34 minutes

---

## Background

Jordan is a software developer at a mid-size tech company in Austin. He's been living on his own for 2 years and recently started thinking seriously about buying his first home. He opened a FinTrack account after a friend recommended it. Before FinTrack, he tracked spending in a spreadsheet he made himself.

---

## Interview

**Q: Tell me about your financial situation before you started using FinTrack.**

Jordan: "Total chaos, honestly. I had a spreadsheet I tried to keep up with, but I'd forget to enter transactions for like two weeks and then give up. I knew roughly what I was spending but had no real system. I kept pushing off saving for a house because I never felt like I had a clear picture of where things stood."

**Q: What made you try FinTrack specifically?**

Jordan: "My coworker Kyle mentioned it. He said the bank sync was actually reliable, which I was skeptical about. I'd tried Mint years ago and it was always disconnecting or miscategorizing everything. Figured I'd give FinTrack a shot since it was cheap."

**Q: How's the bank sync working for you?**

Jordan: "Way better than I expected. I have three accounts - checking, savings, and a credit card - and they all stay connected. Occasionally my credit card will need to re-authenticate, like every couple months, but it sends me a notification so I know to fix it. It's not perfect but it's good enough that I actually trust the data."

**Q: What do you use FinTrack for day to day?**

Jordan: "The spending breakdown is where I live. I look at it probably 3-4 times a week. I want to know how much I'm spending on food, on subscriptions, on going out. The AI categorization is mostly good - it catches stuff I'd forget to categorize. Once in a while it puts a restaurant on Uber Eats under 'transportation' instead of 'food' but it's usually right."

**Q: You mentioned saving for a house. How does FinTrack help with that?**

Jordan: "That's actually the main reason I stuck with it. I set up a savings goal for my down payment and it shows me progress toward it. What I really want, though, is the savings rate dashboard. It tells me what percentage of my income I'm actually saving each month. That number has become kind of a north star for me - I want to keep it above 20%. Before FinTrack I had no idea what my savings rate was."

**Q: What would make FinTrack more useful for you?**

Jordan: "Better investment tracking, honestly. I have a 401k and a Robinhood account, but FinTrack doesn't pull in the Robinhood transactions cleanly. The 401k shows up in net worth but it doesn't show me performance or holdings breakdown. I want to see all my investments in one place with real numbers - like how am I actually doing versus a benchmark."

**Q: How's the mobile experience?**

Jordan: "I use the app a lot actually. Checking my spending when I'm out to dinner to see if I'm over budget for the month. The transaction feed is fast. But sometimes the categorization takes a while to update on mobile. Like I'll make a purchase and it shows as 'uncategorized' for hours. Would be nice if that was faster."

**Q: Any features you wish existed?**

Jordan: "A first home buying checklist or guide would be cool - like, here's how much you need, here's how to think about your savings rate and timeline. Nothing fancy, just FinTrack-specific context. Also, my girlfriend and I are starting to think about combining finances eventually, and I have no idea how that would work in FinTrack."

**Q: How do you feel about the $9.99 price?**

Jordan: "Honestly? Worth it. I've cut probably $200 a month in spending I didn't realize I was doing - random subscriptions, too much eating out. It paid for itself in the first month."

---

## Key Insights
- Bank sync reliability was a key adoption driver (past negative experience with Mint)
- Savings rate dashboard is high-value feature for goal-oriented users
- Savings goal / down payment tracking drives retention
- Investment tracking is a gap - wants consolidated view with performance data
- Mobile transaction feed used frequently; AI categorization delay frustrating
- Interest in shared finances / couples feature as life stage evolves
- Strong ROI perception - concrete dollar savings exceed subscription cost
