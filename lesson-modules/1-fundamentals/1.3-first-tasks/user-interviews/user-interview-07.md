# User Interview #7: Couple Managing Shared Finances Persona

**Participant:** Taylor and Marcus Henderson
**Roles:** Taylor - UX Researcher (29); Marcus - Physical Therapist (31)
**FinTrack Plan:** Monthly ($9.99/mo)
**Date:** February 17, 2025
**Interviewer:** Senior PM
**Duration:** 38 minutes (joint interview)

---

## Background

Taylor and Marcus moved in together 18 months ago. They're not married but share an apartment and split most expenses. They have separate checking accounts and a joint account for shared bills. They've been using FinTrack for four months, mainly trying to get visibility across both their finances and shared household costs.

---

## Interview

**Q: Tell me about your financial setup as a couple.**

Taylor: "It's kind of complicated by design. We each keep our own accounts for personal spending. We have a joint checking for rent, utilities, groceries - household stuff. I connected my personal accounts plus the joint account in FinTrack. Marcus has his own FinTrack account. We're trying to coordinate but it doesn't really work smoothly."

Marcus: "Yeah, it's like we have two separate financial views with no bridge between them. Taylor will ask me how much I spent on something and I have to check my app and tell her. There's no way for us to see each other's data in one place without fully merging accounts, which we don't want to do."

**Q: What would a proper shared finances setup look like for you?**

Taylor: "We should be able to link our accounts so we can both see a household view - our joint expenses together - while keeping our personal spending separate. Like, 'here's the household budget' and 'here's my personal spending' as two different views, but both accessible to both of us."

Marcus: "Exactly. And we should both be able to set budgets on the shared stuff. Right now Taylor does all the budget management because it's her account. I feel like a passenger."

**Q: How do you currently handle household budget conversations?**

Marcus: "We have a monthly check-in where Taylor pulls up her FinTrack and shows me the joint account transactions. It's better than guessing, but it feels like she's the accountant presenting to me, not us managing it together."

Taylor: "And honestly I don't love that dynamic either. I don't want to be the household CFO. I want us to have equal ownership of this. The way FinTrack is set up, someone has to be the 'owner' and everyone else is secondary."

**Q: What's working well in your current setup?**

Taylor: "I love the AI spending categories. I'm kind of a data nerd and the breakdowns are really satisfying. Seeing 'you spent X on dining out this month' with the actual breakdown is exactly what I want. And the bill calendar is fantastic - we have so many recurring bills between two people in one apartment."

Marcus: "I use the app mainly to track my personal spending goals. I have a savings goal for a ski trip and it's fun to watch the progress bar. The credit score monitoring is also cool - I hadn't checked my score in probably two years."

**Q: What's most frustrating?**

Taylor: "The lack of real shared account functionality. It's the thing that's been bugging me since we signed up. And I've seen it mentioned in FinTrack's Reddit community too - couples dealing with this."

Marcus: "The mobile notifications for me. I set up some budget alerts but I get a notification for literally every transaction. Like, I know I bought a coffee, I was there. I don't need a push notification telling me what I just did. I want alerts only when I'm approaching or over a budget limit, not for every transaction."

**Q: Have you considered just merging into one account?**

Taylor: "We talked about it and decided not to. We're not married. We both want financial independence. The whole point is to have shared visibility without losing our individual financial identities. FinTrack treats it as all or nothing."

**Q: What features would you most want?**

Taylor: "Household account that supports two equal co-owners with separate personal account views. That's the main one. Second would be better notification controls - let me configure exactly which events trigger alerts."

Marcus: "For me, better investment tracking. I have a Fidelity account and it shows up in FinTrack but I can't see my fund breakdown or performance. It's just a number. I'd want more depth there."

**Q: Would you be willing to pay more for a proper couples/household plan?**

Taylor: "Absolutely. If it solved the shared finances problem properly, I'd pay $15 a month. Maybe $20. This is something we'd probably use for years, and financial arguments are genuinely stressful. A tool that reduces that stress is worth paying for."

---

## Key Insights
- Couples without merged finances are an underserved segment - want shared household view with individual privacy
- Current single-owner model creates unequal dynamic; one person defaults to "household CFO" role
- Per-transaction notifications are too noisy - users want threshold-based alerts only
- Both users want to co-manage budgets with equal access/permissions
- Household bill calendar works well across combined recurring expenses
- Investment tracking depth is lacking (both users mentioned independently)
- Willingness to pay is higher for a true household/couples plan tier
- Reddit community awareness suggests this pain point is widely felt
