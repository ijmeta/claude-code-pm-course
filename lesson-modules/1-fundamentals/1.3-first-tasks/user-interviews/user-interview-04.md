# User Interview #4: Freelancer / Irregular Income Persona

**Participant:** Sofia Mendez
**Role:** Freelance Graphic Designer
**Age:** 32
**FinTrack Plan:** Annual ($79/yr)
**Date:** February 10, 2025
**Interviewer:** Senior PM
**Duration:** 29 minutes

---

## Background

Sofia has been freelancing full-time for four years. She has multiple clients, invoices on net-30 terms, and income that swings wildly month to month. She's been using FinTrack for about a year, having tried Mint and YNAB before. She's technically comfortable and opinionated.

---

## Interview

**Q: What's unique about managing finances as a freelancer compared to someone with a regular salary?**

Sofia: "Everything. I might get $3,000 one month and $9,000 the next. I might get paid for November work in January because of net-30. My expenses don't match when my income arrives. The whole 'set a monthly budget and track against it' model assumes you have the same income every month. That doesn't work for me."

**Q: How do you use FinTrack given that model?**

Sofia: "I've kind of hacked it. I separate business income from personal income manually since FinTrack doesn't really understand freelance accounting. I look at trailing 3-month averages more than monthly totals. I set budgets conservatively based on my slowest months. It works, but I'm always doing mental gymnastics."

**Q: Tell me about the bank sync experience.**

Sofia: "Mixed. My main checking account works great. My business checking - I have a separate Chase Business account - keeps disconnecting. Like every six weeks I have to go back in and reconnect it. It's annoying because then I have a gap in my transaction history. I've talked to support about it and they said it was a Plaid issue but it keeps happening."

**Q: What features do you rely on most?**

Sofia: "The net worth tracker. Because my income is lumpy, month-to-month budgeting stresses me out. Net worth going up is the signal I actually care about. I check it every couple weeks. Is the trend line going up? Great. It smooths out the noise of the irregular income. The savings rate dashboard is similar - I care more about the 6-month trend than any single month."

**Q: What's frustrating or missing?**

Sofia: "No way to handle variable income properly. Like, FinTrack sees my income as 'low' in lean months and flags my savings rate as terrible, but doesn't understand that I have $15k sitting in a business account from a big project that I'm drawing from. Also, the AI categorization is really not built for freelancers. It'll categorize my Adobe subscription as 'entertainment' but it's a business expense. I need a business-expense flag that's separate from categories."

**Q: You mentioned you tried YNAB. Why did you leave?**

Sofia: "YNAB's philosophy is great - give every dollar a job - but the manual entry burden is too high. I'd spend 20 minutes a week just maintaining it. FinTrack's bank sync means I can spend 5 minutes a week instead. The automation is worth the trade-off even if the mental model is less clean."

**Q: What would a FinTrack feature built for freelancers look like?**

Sofia: "Income smoothing would be the first thing. Like, take my last 12 months of income, divide by 12, that's my 'effective monthly income' for budgeting purposes. And a way to tag transactions as business expenses for tax purposes. Even just a checkbox that says 'deductible' would help. Right now I export everything to a spreadsheet in January and do this manually."

**Q: How about the mobile experience?**

Sofia: "I use it on my iPad more than my phone. The bigger screen is nicer for the net worth charts. The phone app is fine but the charts feel cramped on a small screen. I'd love to be able to annotate transactions - like add a note that says 'retainer payment from Client X' so I remember what the deposit was for."

**Q: What would make you recommend FinTrack to other freelancers?**

Sofia: "If it actually understood irregular income. Right now I recommend it with caveats - 'it's great but you have to ignore some of the month-to-month stuff.' That's not ideal. If they built real freelancer support, it'd be a no-brainer."

---

## Key Insights
- Monthly budget model is a poor fit for freelancers with irregular income
- Net worth tracker and savings rate trends are more useful than monthly budgets for this segment
- Business account bank sync (Chase Business) has recurring disconnection issues
- AI categorization doesn't distinguish business expenses from personal - big gap for freelancers
- Transaction annotation / note-adding is a missing feature
- Income smoothing (rolling average) would be a valuable freelancer-specific feature
- YNAB comparison: FinTrack wins on automation, loses on budgeting methodology
- Tablet usage pattern - screen real estate matters for chart-heavy views
