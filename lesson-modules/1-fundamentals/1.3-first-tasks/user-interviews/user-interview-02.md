# User Interview #2: Parent / Family Budgeter Persona

**Participant:** Michelle Okafor
**Role:** Elementary School Teacher
**Age:** 35
**FinTrack Plan:** Annual ($79/yr)
**Date:** February 5, 2025
**Interviewer:** Senior PM
**Duration:** 31 minutes

---

## Background

Michelle and her husband Dayo have two kids (ages 6 and 9). She manages most of the household finances. Before FinTrack, they used a shared Google Sheet that Dayo rarely updated. She switched to FinTrack about 8 months ago after getting frustrated with the spreadsheet constantly falling out of date.

---

## Interview

**Q: What was managing finances like before FinTrack?**

Michelle: "I was basically doing it all myself. I'd update the spreadsheet, Dayo would forget to log things, then we'd argue about why we were over budget. He'd spend $80 at Costco and just not tell me, and the numbers would be off. The spreadsheet required both of us to actually use it, which was the problem."

**Q: How did FinTrack change that dynamic?**

Michelle: "The bank sync is the key. Since everything connects automatically, neither of us has to remember to log transactions. The money leaves the account and it shows up in FinTrack. Dayo actually looks at it now because he doesn't have to do any work. We have a Sunday routine where we review the week together. That's something we never did before."

**Q: Walk me through how you use budget goals.**

Michelle: "I set up categories for everything - groceries, eating out, kids activities, gas. Each one has a monthly budget. The progress bars are really helpful - I can see at a glance if we're at 80% of our grocery budget on the 15th and course correct. Before FinTrack, I'd only find out we went over at the end of the month when it was too late."

**Q: What's not working well for you?**

Michelle: "The shared account setup is confusing. Dayo and I both have the app, but it's really my account that he can view. There's no real concept of a household account with two equal owners. I want us to both be able to set budgets together, not have it be 'Michelle's FinTrack that Dayo has read access to.' It makes me feel like I'm the financial manager and he's just along for the ride."

**Q: Tell me more about what a true household budget feature would look like.**

Michelle: "We should both be able to add notes to transactions, both be able to adjust budgets. Right now if Dayo tags something wrong I have to fix it. And we should both get the alert notifications when we're close to a budget limit. He only finds out when I tell him, which defeats the purpose."

**Q: What about the bill calendar?**

Michelle: "That I love. Seeing all the upcoming bills on a calendar view is so helpful. Our mortgage, car insurance, streaming subscriptions - it's all there. I had no idea how many subscriptions we had until FinTrack surfaced them. We cancelled three we forgot about. That was like $45 a month we were just throwing away."

**Q: How do you handle irregular income or expenses?**

Michelle: "We have pretty steady income since teaching salary is consistent. But there's always stuff like back-to-school shopping or holiday spending that throws off the budgets. I wish I could set up seasonal budgets - like, increase the clothing budget in August without messing up my usual monthly numbers."

**Q: What features would you most want added?**

Michelle: "Equal household accounts - that's number one. Two real users, same account, same permissions. Number two is probably better notification controls. Right now every overage alert goes to my phone. I want those to go to both of us. We both need to know when we're over on eating out, not just me."

**Q: How does the subscription price feel?**

Michelle: "We switched to annual to save a few dollars. At $79 a year I don't even think about it. The subscription alerts alone have saved us way more than that."

---

## Key Insights
- Automatic bank sync solves the "partner doesn't update the spreadsheet" problem
- Household/shared account feature is a major gap - permissions model feels unequal
- Dual notification delivery for both household members needed
- Budget progress bars are highly valued for real-time awareness
- Bill calendar and subscription discovery are standout features
- Seasonal budget flexibility would be useful for families
- Annual plan adoption driven by cost savings; strong price-value perception
