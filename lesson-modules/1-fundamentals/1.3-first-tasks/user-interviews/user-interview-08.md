# User Interview #8: Small Business Owner Using FinTrack for Personal Finances

**Participant:** Andre Williams
**Role:** Owner, Williams Electrical Services (solo + 2 employees)
**Age:** 41
**FinTrack Plan:** Annual ($79/yr)
**Date:** February 19, 2025
**Interviewer:** Senior PM
**Duration:** 32 minutes

---

## Background

Andre owns a small electrical contracting business. He has a business checking account and a personal checking account that are thoroughly intermingled - he pays himself erratically, sometimes transfers money to cover personal expenses, and often runs personal purchases through the business card. He uses FinTrack strictly for personal finances and has a separate accountant for the business side. He's been a FinTrack user for 11 months.

---

## Interview

**Q: How do you separate personal and business finances in FinTrack?**

Andre: "Honestly? I don't do a great job of it. I have my personal accounts in FinTrack and my business accounts are supposed to stay out of it. But sometimes I pay for a personal thing on my business card and then I get confused when it shows up or doesn't show up. I've excluded the business accounts from FinTrack but sometimes I forget which card I used."

**Q: How does your variable income work in the app?**

Andre: "My 'paycheck' is whatever I transfer from the business account to my personal account each month. It's different every month depending on how business is going. So FinTrack sees very irregular income and my savings rate is all over the place. In a good month I'll transfer $8,000 to myself and it looks like I'm crushing it. In a slow month it might be $3,500 and FinTrack acts like I'm in crisis."

**Q: What value does FinTrack provide given all that complexity?**

Andre: "The net worth tracker is the main thing. Everything in one place - my personal savings, my home equity, my retirement accounts. I check it once a month. I want to know: is my net worth going up or down? That's the real signal. The monthly income noise is less important to me."

**Q: What's your biggest frustration with the app?**

Andre: "Bank sync reliability. My business bank is a local credit union, and the connection breaks constantly. Like, every two to three weeks it disconnects. I've had to manually enter transactions multiple times. I know FinTrack uses Plaid and some banks aren't well-supported, but it makes me feel like I can't trust the data."

**Q: Tell me about how you use the spending side of FinTrack.**

Andre: "I have some categories set up for personal stuff. But the AI categorization gets confused because some of my transactions look like business expenses. Like, I'll buy something at Home Depot for my house and it gets categorized as 'business supplies.' Or I'll buy a personal tool and it's unclear. The app doesn't have a way for me to say 'this is definitely personal, even though it looks business-y.'"

**Q: Have you looked at the bill calendar?**

Andre: "That's actually really useful for me. I have personal bills and some business expenses I pay personally - my truck insurance, my tools subscription, my accountant. Seeing it all in a calendar keeps me from getting surprised by big bills. I also set up credit card due date alerts which have saved me from late fees a couple times."

**Q: What about savings goals?**

Andre: "I set up a goal for a vacation. That one works fine. But I'd like to set goals that are more like 'emergency fund for slow months' - like, I want 3 months of personal expenses saved separately from my business float. FinTrack has the goal tools but they're not quite right for that use case. I want a goal linked to my expense average, not a fixed dollar amount."

**Q: What would a small business owner version of FinTrack look like to you?**

Andre: "Let me tag transactions as personal vs. business even from the same account. If I could mark that Home Depot run as personal, it would flow into my personal budgets correctly. Also, a smarter income view for people who pay themselves irregularly - maybe a rolling average or a way to smooth out the lumpy months. My accountant handles the real business financials. I just want my personal picture to be clean."

**Q: Any other feedback?**

Andre: "The credit score tracking is great - I use credit carefully because my business sometimes needs financing, so my personal score matters. That feature alone is probably worth the $79. I just wish the bank sync was more reliable. That's the thing that makes me feel like I can't fully trust the app."

---

## Key Insights
- Business/personal account mixing is common for small business owners - a meaningful user segment without a clean solution
- Net worth tracker is primary value driver; less reliant on monthly spending tracking
- Bank sync reliability with local credit unions and smaller banks is a significant issue
- AI categorization is confused by business-looking personal transactions - needs manual override or explicit tagging
- Irregular income creates noise in savings rate and budgets - same problem as freelancers
- Bill calendar and payment alerts deliver clear, concrete value
- Expense-relative savings goals (e.g., 3 months of expenses) not currently supported
- Credit score monitoring valued by small business owners who monitor personal credit for business financing
