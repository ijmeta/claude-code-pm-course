## Competitive Landscape

### Direct Competitors

**YNAB (You Need a Budget):**
- Strengths: Loyal community, strong budgeting methodology
- Weaknesses: Steep learning curve, no investment tracking, $14.99/mo
- Our differentiation: Easier onboarding, holistic net worth view, AI categorization

**Monarch Money:**
- Strengths: Clean UI, couples/family features, investment tracking
- Weaknesses: $14.99/mo price point, no credit score monitoring
- Our differentiation: Lower price, credit score + bill calendar, stronger AI

**Copilot:**
- Strengths: Beautiful design, Apple-ecosystem focus
- Weaknesses: iOS only, $13/mo, no Android, no web
- Our differentiation: Cross-platform, credit monitoring, wider feature set

**Credit Karma / Empower:**
- Strengths: Free, massive user base
- Weaknesses: Ad-driven model, data monetization, surface-level insights
- Our differentiation: Ad-free, privacy-first, deeper budgeting tools

### The Mint Opportunity

Mint shut down in January 2024, displacing ~3.6 million active users overnight. FinTrack aggressively targeted Mint refugees and acquired ~40,000 subscribers in Q1 2024 alone. This validated the market demand for a paid, trustworthy alternative.

## Product Philosophy

### Core Beliefs

**1. Clarity over data overload**
- More numbers doesn't mean more insight
- Highlight what matters, hide what doesn't
- Actionable insights beat raw data

**2. Automation first**
- Manual categorization is a tax on the user's time
- Smart defaults should be right 90%+ of the time
- Let people override, not do the work from scratch

**3. Honest about money**
- Don't shame spending; help people understand it
- Surface trends gently - coaching tone, not alarm tone
- Progress over perfection

**4. Security is the product**
- Users share their most sensitive data with us
- Bank-level encryption, read-only connections, no credential storage
- Security and trust are core features, not just table stakes

**5. Whole picture thinking**
- Budget + net worth + credit + bills together
- Decisions affect everything - the product should show that
- A raise affects savings rate, debt payoff speed, and net worth simultaneously

## Key Terminology

**Account:** A connected financial account (bank, credit card, investment, loan)
**Transaction:** An individual financial event pulled from a connected account
**Category:** The spending/income label applied to a transaction (e.g., "Groceries," "Salary")
**Budget Goal:** A monthly spending target set by the user for a category or group
**Net Worth:** Total assets minus total liabilities, calculated across all connected accounts
**Bank Sync:** Automated connection to financial institutions via Plaid
**Savings Rate:** Percentage of income saved each month (income minus expenses / income)
**Bill Calendar:** Visual calendar of upcoming recurring bills and subscriptions
**Snowball Method:** Debt payoff strategy prioritizing smallest balances first
**Avalanche Method:** Debt payoff strategy prioritizing highest interest rates first
