# FinTrack — Company Case Study

All course modules take place inside **FinTrack**, a fictional Series B personal finance SaaS startup. This folder contains the company reference materials that Claude uses throughout the course to give exercises real-world context.

You don't need to read these files upfront — Claude will reference them during each module as needed. But they're here if you want to explore the company on your own.

---

## The Company

**FinTrack** — *"See your full financial picture, finally"*

FinTrack is a personal finance and budgeting app often described as "Mint meets YNAB, but smarter." It syncs with bank accounts via Plaid, automatically categorizes transactions, and helps users set and track financial goals.

| Metric | Value (Q1 2026) |
|--------|----------------|
| Active paid subscribers | 180,000 |
| ARR | $18M |
| MRR | $1.5M |
| Monthly growth | 8% MoM |
| Annual retention | 85% |
| NPS | 52 |
| Funding | Series B, $18M raised |
| HQ | Austin, TX |
| Team size | 45 people |

**Pricing:** $9.99/month or $79/year (62% on annual plan)

## Files

### `COMPANY.md`
Full company background: founding story, team structure, funding history, key metrics, strategic priorities, and the product roadmap.

### `PRODUCT.md`
Detailed product documentation: features, key differentiators, pricing tiers, technical integrations (Plaid), and the near-term roadmap.

### `PERSONAS.md`
Three detailed user personas used throughout the course:

- **Sarah the Saver** — early-career professional, focused on paying off debt and building an emergency fund
- **Marcus the Manager** — mid-career, managing household finances for a family, needs oversight not micromanagement
- **Lisa the Lifestyle** — high earner, lifestyle creep concerns, wants to spend guilt-free within a plan

### `COMPETITIVE.md`
Competitive landscape analysis covering: Mint, YNAB, Credit Karma, Empower, and NerdWallet — including feature comparisons, positioning, and FinTrack's differentiation.
