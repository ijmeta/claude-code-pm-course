# FinTrack Product Overview

**Your complete guide to the FinTrack product**

---

## What is FinTrack?

FinTrack is a personal finance and budgeting SaaS for individuals, couples, and households. Think **"Mint done right"** - the breadth of Mint, the polish of Copilot, the depth of YNAB, but at a price and experience that makes it accessible to everyone.

### Core Value Proposition

**For people who feel overwhelmed or in the dark about their money,** FinTrack is a personal finance platform **that automatically syncs all your accounts, categorizes spending with AI, and gives you a clear picture of your budget, net worth, and financial progress.** Unlike Mint (shut down) or YNAB (steep learning curve and expensive), FinTrack **gets you to financial clarity in minutes, not hours - for $9.99/month.**

---

## Product Philosophy

### Key Principles

**1. Automatic by default**
- Connect your bank and FinTrack does the work
- AI categorizes transactions without manual effort
- Bills detected automatically from transaction history
- Net worth calculated continuously across all accounts

**2. Clarity over comprehensiveness**
- Show what matters, hide what doesn't
- One number for net worth, one chart for spending trends
- Progressive disclosure - simple surface, depth when wanted
- Actionable insights beat data dumps

**3. Honest and non-judgmental**
- Spending is personal - FinTrack informs, never shames
- Coaching tone in all copy ("Here's a trend" not "You overspent!")
- Celebrate wins (savings milestones, debt payoffs)
- Progress over perfection

**4. Privacy and trust first**
- Read-only connections to financial institutions
- No credential storage (Plaid tokenization)
- Zero ads, zero data selling - subscription model only
- Transparent about what data is used and why

**5. Whole financial picture**
- Budget + net worth + credit score + bill calendar together
- Every feature aware of the others (budget goal affects net worth projection)
- One app, not five

## Core Features

### 1. Bank Sync (via Plaid)

**What users can do:**
- Connect checking, savings, credit cards, investment accounts, loans, mortgages
- Automatic transaction import (daily sync, manual refresh available)
- Support for 12,000+ US financial institutions
- Multi-account household view (connect all family accounts)

**Our differentiation:**
- **Plaid-powered:** Industry-standard connectivity, not screen-scraping
- **Read-only access:** FinTrack can never initiate transactions
- **Reconnect prompts:** Smart alerts when a bank connection breaks, with guided fix flow
- **Historical import:** Pull up to 2 years of transaction history on initial connect

---

### 2. AI Spending Categorization

**What users can do:**
- View all transactions automatically categorized (Groceries, Dining, Utilities, etc.)
- Correct miscategorized transactions with one tap
- Split transactions across multiple categories
- Create custom categories and sub-categories
- Set category rules (e.g., "Always categorize Whole Foods as Groceries")

**Our differentiation:**
- **ML-powered:** Model trained on 50M+ anonymized transactions
- **Personalization:** Learns individual spending patterns over time
- **Merchant intelligence:** Recognizes 250,000+ merchants by name
- **Current accuracy:** 82% auto-categorization (target: 91%)
- **User corrections improve the model:** Feedback loop improves future suggestions

---

### 3. Budget Goals

**What users can do:**
- Set monthly spending limits by category (e.g., $400 for Dining)
- View real-time progress toward each budget goal
- Rollover unused budget to next month (optional)
- Set shared budget goals for household accounts
- Get alerts when approaching or exceeding a budget limit

**Our differentiation:**
- **Smart goal suggestions:** Based on last 3 months of spending history
- **Flexible periods:** Monthly, weekly, or custom date range budgets
- **Goal summaries:** Monthly recap with trend vs. prior months
- **Collaborative budgets:** Couples can view and edit shared goals together

---

### 4. Net Worth Tracker

**What users can do:**
- View total net worth (all assets minus all liabilities) in one number
- See net worth trend over time (7 days, 30 days, 1 year, all time)
- Track assets: bank accounts, investments, property (manual entry)
- Track liabilities: credit cards, student loans, auto loans, mortgages
- Set net worth milestones and celebrate hitting them

**Our differentiation:**
- **Real-time updates:** Recalculates whenever any connected account syncs
- **Milestone moments:** App celebrates hitting $10K, $50K, $100K net worth
- **Investment account support:** Shows investment balances without trade-level detail
- **Debt payoff impact:** Shows how paying off a loan improves net worth

---

### 5. Bill Calendar

**What users can do:**
- View upcoming bills and subscriptions in a calendar view
- See which bills are due in the next 7, 14, or 30 days
- Mark bills as paid (or auto-detected from bank sync)
- Get push/email notifications before bills are due
- Track recurring subscriptions (Netflix, Spotify, gym, etc.)

**Our differentiation:**
- **Auto-detection:** Bills identified from recurring transaction patterns - no manual entry
- **Never miss a payment:** Dashboard widget for "Due This Week"
- **Subscription audit:** Shows all detected subscriptions with monthly total cost
- **Bill vs. discretionary split:** Shows fixed expenses vs. variable spending

---

### 6. Credit Score Monitoring

**What users can do:**
- View current credit score (VantageScore 3.0 via TransUnion)
- Track credit score history over time
- See key factors affecting credit score (payment history, utilization, etc.)
- Get alerts when score changes significantly
- View credit utilization by card

**Our differentiation:**
- **Included in subscription:** No separate sign-up or credit card required
- **Score change alerts:** Notified within 24 hours of a significant change
- **Contextual explanations:** Plain-English explanation of what's affecting the score
- **Utilization per card:** Shows which specific cards are hurting or helping utilization

---

### 7. Savings Rate Dashboard

**What users can do:**
- View current month savings rate (income minus expenses divided by income)
- Track savings rate trend over 3, 6, and 12 months
- Set a savings rate goal (e.g., "Save 20% of income")
- See income vs. total spending breakdown
- View month-over-month savings comparison

**Our differentiation:**
- **Automatic income detection:** Identifies paycheck deposits from transaction history
- **True savings rate:** Accounts for all expenses, not just discretionary
- **Goal tracking:** Progress bar toward user-defined savings rate goal
- **Fire number context:** Optional context for users tracking toward financial independence

## Product Metrics

### North Star Metric

**Monthly Financially-Engaged Users** - Subscribers who connect at least one account AND view their dashboard at least 4 times in a month.

**Why this metric?**
- Indicates genuine ongoing engagement (not just sign-up)
- Correlates with retention (engaged users churn at 3x lower rate)
- Distinguishes active users from dormant subscribers
- Leading indicator of NPS and word-of-mouth

**Current:** ~108,000 monthly financially-engaged users (60% of subscribers)
**Goal (Q4 2026):** 180,000 monthly financially-engaged users

---

### Product Health Metrics

**Activation:**
- **Definition:** User connects at least one bank account AND sets one budget goal within 30 days of signup
- **Current:** 51%
- **Target:** 65%
- **Why it matters:** Activated users have 4x higher 12-month retention

**Retention:**
- **Definition:** % of subscribers who renew at next billing cycle
- **Current:** 85% annual, 91% monthly
- **Target:** 88% annual
- **Why it matters:** Retention is the core driver of ARR growth

**Time to First Insight:**
- **Definition:** Time from sign-up to viewing a personalized spending insight
- **Current:** 22 minutes (median, after first bank sync completes)
- **Target:** Under 10 minutes
- **Why it matters:** Faster value = higher activation and lower early churn

**AI Categorization Accuracy:**
- **Definition:** % of transactions auto-categorized correctly (no user correction needed)
- **Current:** 82%
- **Target:** 91%
- **Why it matters:** #1 driver of support tickets; accuracy = trust

## Product Principles in Action

### Example: How We Built Bill Calendar

**Principle: Automatic by default**
- Bills detected from recurring transaction patterns - zero manual entry needed
- Users see their bill calendar populated on first login (not blank)
- Handles irregular billing (quarterly, annual) not just monthly

**Principle: Clarity over comprehensiveness**
- One view: what's due in the next 30 days, ordered by date
- Color-coded: overdue (red), due soon (yellow), paid (green)
- Total upcoming expense shown prominently ("$847 due in the next 30 days")

**Principle: Honest and non-judgmental**
- Subscription audit shows full list without commentary
- No "you're spending too much on subscriptions" - just the data
- Users decide what to cut, FinTrack just makes it visible

**Result:** Bill calendar is the #2 most-used feature after transaction view (used by 71% of active subscribers monthly)
